Why FundMentor exists

FundMentor was built to make portfolio work clearer. Not just to surface investments, but to help users think through fit, concentration, overlap, tax awareness, and how to explain a portfolio with confidence.

For information and education only — not investment advice.

What FundMentor is

FundMentor is a portfolio planning and review application designed for advisors, wealth teams, and serious investors who want more structure in how they build, review, and explain portfolios.

The current version focuses on ETFs, with support for model building, idea generation, portfolio diagnostics, and rebalancing. The broader direction is to apply the same type of data-driven logic across more investment products over time.

Why it was built

A lot of investment tools do one of two things: they either look polished but stay shallow, or they are powerful but hard to interpret in real conversations. FundMentor was built to bridge that gap.

The goal is not only to help users find ideas. It is to help them understand what a portfolio is doing, where it is strong, where it deserves attention, and how to speak about it more clearly.

How it works

  • Plan — shape an allocation and build a shortlist with clearer guardrails.
  • Ideas — compare candidates and explore the broader ETF universe.
  • Portfolio — review strengths, watch items, overlap, concentration, and fit.
  • Rebalance — compare current holdings to target and identify what needs attention.

Who it is for

  • Independent advisors and RIAs
  • Wealth teams and model portfolio groups
  • Asset managers exploring structured portfolio logic
  • Serious DIY investors who want more than a screener

What makes it different

FundMentor is not just trying to rank products. It is designed to help users think through allocation, suitability, diagnostics, and explanation in one flow.

Data & privacy

Portfolio data used in the Portfolio and Rebalance workflows is processed in-session and is not stored by FundMentor.

Roadmap
